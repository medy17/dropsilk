Security by Design

End-to-End Encryption

All data transferred between you and your peer, including files and screen shares, is encrypted end-to-end using industry-standard WebRTC protocols (DTLS and SRTP). This means the data is encrypted on the sender's device and can only be decrypted by the recipient's device. No one in between—not even the DropSilk server—can access the contents of your transfer.

No Cloud Storage Risk

Your files are never uploaded to or stored on a central server. The transfer happens directly between the browsers of the connected peers. This eliminates the risk of a traditional cloud data breach exposing your files, as there is no central repository to attack.

Ephemeral Sessions

Each "flight" is a temporary, private session. The connection information is held by our server only for the duration of the session. Once you or your peer disconnects, the session is terminated and the connection data is discarded.

Your Responsibility

While we provide a secure channel, your security also depends on you. Always ensure you are sharing files with a person you know and trust. We recommend verifying the flight code with them through a separate, secure channel (like a private message) before initiating a transfer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a file size limit?

Theoretically, no. DropSilk is designed to handle large files by streaming them directly. However, performance for extremely large files (many gigabytes) can be affected by your device's memory capacity and the stability of your network connection.

Why is my transfer speed slow?

Transfer speed is determined by the direct network path between you and your peer. For the fastest speeds, ensure both devices are on the same local Wi-Fi network (LAN). When transferring over the internet (WAN), the speed is limited by the slowest of the two users' internet connections (specifically, the sender's upload speed and the receiver's download speed).

Is DropSilk free to use?

Yes, DropSilk is completely free. It was created as a passion project to offer a simple and private file sharing solution.

What browsers are supported?

DropSilk works on all modern, up-to-date desktop browsers that fully support WebRTC, such as Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, and Safari. DropSilk may look slightly different on some browsers due to the way they choose to interpret certain parts of the CSS spec. This does not affect functionality and is purely aesthetic.

What happens if I get disconnected?

If your network connection is interrupted, the file transfer will stop. You will need to reconnect to the flight and restart the transfer for any incomplete files. There is currently no functionality to resume a failed transfer. Fully received files will remain accessible and downloadable until you close the tab.

How does screen sharing work?

Screen sharing uses the same secure, peer-to-peer WebRTC technology as file transfers. Your screen is encrypted and streamed directly to your peer, and is never visible to our servers.
